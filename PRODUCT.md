# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Fans arriving at a FIFA World Cup 2026 venue, volunteers helping them move through the stadium, and venue operators coordinating safety, access, transport, and incident response. Fans need fast, understandable answers in their language; staff need an at-a-glance operational picture and actionable next steps.

## Product Purpose

ArenaMind is a GenAI-enabled stadium operations and fan-experience command platform. It connects live venue signals, accessible wayfinding, transit context, multilingual assistance, incident triage, staff dispatch, emergency broadcasting, and sustainability rewards in one workspace. Success means a fan can make a confident decision in seconds and an operator can turn a signal into a coordinated action without switching tools.

## Brand Personality

Calm, capable, human. The voice is direct and reassuring under pressure, with enough energy to feel like a live tournament product. It should make complex venue conditions feel legible rather than dramatic.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic dark-mode SaaS dashboards, neon AI gradients, fake security theater, dense wall-to-wall cards, decorative map grids, vague hype language, and sports-broadcast clutter. Do not make accessibility an afterthought or hide key actions behind unexplained icons.

## Design Principles

- Put the next useful decision in reach: every signal should lead to a clear action or answer.
- Show the venue as a living place: use spatial context, movement, and real operational language.
- Reduce pressure through clarity: prioritize status, impact, and recommended action over decoration.
- Make inclusion operational: accessibility, language, and readable controls belong in the main flow.
- Keep trust visible and honest: distinguish simulated data, AI suggestions, and confirmed operator actions.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Support keyboard navigation, visible focus states, readable contrast, text scaling, high contrast, reduced motion, multilingual labels, and screen-reader-friendly status announcements. Never make color the only way to understand crowd or incident severity.
